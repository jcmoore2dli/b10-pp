1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This document specifies the scaffold configuration system for Extended Supported
Opinion (ESO) tasks in the B10 Practice Platform (B10-PP). The scaffold allows
instructors to configure AI scoring and feedback focus for individual task
assignments targeting ILR 2+ to ILR 3 speaking proficiency.

Scope: ESO tasks only. ILR 3 is the performance target. ILR 2 is the assumed
floor. ILR 2 constructs (basic narrative, description, instruction-giving) are
explicitly out of scope as scoring criteria.

Task definition: The student states and defends a position on a complex or
abstract issue — policy debates, competing values, stakeholder implications,
societal tradeoffs. Examples include government regulation of digital platforms,
universal basic income, environmental policy, military readiness policy, and
healthcare access.

PEDAGOGIC RATIONALE: The scaffold serves two functions. For the learner, it
isolates sub-skills for focused practice — a learner cannot improve everything
at once, and a tractable target produces faster, more visible gains. For the
platform, it makes explicit that B10-PP teaches argumentation and reasoning
language in context — how to construct a position, how to deploy a discourse
frame, how to realize complex grammatical structures in service of an argument.
This is transferable language function, not test preparation.

NOTE ON SCOPE DIFFERENCE FROM ILR 2 TASKS: The ESO scaffold operates at a
higher construct level than the narration, description, and instructions
scaffolds. The ILR 2 scaffolds isolate sub-skills within a single functional
task type. The ESO scaffold isolates sub-skills within argumentation and
academic discourse — a multi-dimensional construct that involves reasoning
moves, discourse frames, grammatical complexity, and argument structure
simultaneously. The scaffold architecture reflects this complexity.

2. DESIGN PRINCIPLES

- Fixed infrastructure and instructor-configurable focus are strictly separated.
  Construct-critical rules are never instructor-modifiable.

- Primary focus drives feedback priority. The scorer leads with the
  instructor-selected dimension. Scaffold selection affects feedback weighting
  only — never scoring logic.

- SCAFFOLD INVARIANT: The holistic score is always produced against the full
  rubric regardless of scaffold selection. Scaffold focus modifies feedback
  weighting and adds a scaffold_feedback field. It never raises or lowers
  the holistic score. This invariant is non-negotiable and must be enforced
  in the scoring prompt and verified in norming.

- The reasoning gate is always active and never configurable. Selecting
  Argument Structure as a focus mode affects feedback emphasis only — not
  scoring logic. Gate failure (no developed reasoning move present) produces
  Score 2 regardless of focus selection.

- Scaffold focus is diagnostic, not punitive. Weak performance on the scaffold
  target does not lower the holistic score if Level 3 is otherwise functionally
  present. Strong performance on the scaffold target does not raise the holistic
  score if Level 3 is not functionally present.

- REDS status is the evaluation framework for scaffold targets. Every scaffold
  target is evaluated as: Absent / Not Realized / Developing / Sustained.
  These are not quality grades — they are functional performance states relative
  to the Level 3 standard.

- Discourse frames and grammar structures are evaluated for functional Level 3
  realization — not mere presence. A grammar structure that is initiated and
  abandoned, or present but not carrying a reasoning move, is Not Realized.
  A discourse frame that is invoked as a label without developed reasoning
  behind it is Not Realized.

- PISU framework is a student task framework, not a scoring criterion. PISU
  completion must not function as a proxy for Level 3 status in any scoring
  field or scaffold feedback. PISU guidance is developmental coaching only
  and must not appear in the same feedback block as holistic score justification.

- Counterargument is a Score 4 / sustained Level 3 feature. It is not a Score 3
  requirement and must not function as a limiting factor at Score 3. It must not
  appear as a scaffold target requirement at the developing level.

- Lexical range is managed through topic domain selection at task design, not
  through a scaffold focus category.

- Disfluency in ESO tasks is evaluated through transcript-visible signals only
  — false starts revealing structural breakdown at elevation points. Silent pause
  metadata is not passed to the ESO scoring engine. Disfluency is not a scoring
  cap criterion for ESO in the same way it is for ILR 2 tasks — the primary
  disfluency signal is structural collapse at Level 3 ceiling moments, not
  delivery rate.

- Scoring outcomes are determined solely by construct criteria and gate rules.
  Instructor configuration affects feedback only.

3. REDS FRAMEWORK AND SCORING ALIGNMENT

REDS FRAMEWORK — FUNCTIONAL PERFORMANCE STATES

Performance is evaluated using REDS status relative to the Level 3 task target.

Absent:
  The target feature is not present.

Not Realized:
  The target feature is attempted but does not functionally materialize.
  - attempted but not functional
  - invoked but not developed
  - does not carry meaning
  - requires inference from the listener to complete
  - grammatical structure present but reasoning not developed behind it

Developing:
  The target feature is functionally present.
  - functionally present
  - followable from what is explicitly stated
  - inconsistent
  - breaks under pressure or at elevation points
  - present in some places, absent in others

Sustained:
  The target feature is controlled across the full response.
  - controlled and distributed across the response
  - stable under task demands
  - present at elevation points, not only in accessible portions

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SCORING ALIGNMENT — ILR 3 TARGET

Score 4 corresponds to Sustained performance.
Score 3 corresponds to Developing performance — Level 3 functionally present
  but inconsistent. This is the primary instructional target zone.
Score 2 corresponds to Not Realized or Absent — Level 3 not functionally present.
Score 1 is reserved for responses too brief, off-topic, or disfluent to evaluate.

Developing performance does not meet the threshold for Score 4.
Not Realized performance does not meet the threshold for Score 3.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FUNCTIONAL THRESHOLD

A feature is considered functionally realized when it carries meaning in a way
that can be followed from what is explicitly stated, without requiring the
listener to supply unstated logical steps on the student's behalf.

If a feature is present in form but does not carry meaning — grammatical structure
initiated but not completed, reasoning invoked but not developed, discourse frame
named but not deployed — it is classified as Not Realized.

The distinction between Not Realized and Developing is the primary source of
scoring error at the 2/3 boundary. A developed reasoning move must be populated
with enough specificity to be followable. Invocation without population is
Not Realized regardless of grammatical complexity.

4. SCAFFOLD ARCHITECTURE

NOTE ON ARCHITECTURE DIFFERENCES FROM ILR 2 SCAFFOLDS: The ILR 2 scaffolds
(narration, description, instructions) follow a three-layer architecture —
fixed infrastructure, one primary focus, optional monitors. The ESO scaffold
uses a focus mode architecture that reflects the multi-dimensional nature of
argumentation. The instructor selects a focus mode, and for two of those modes
selects a sub-target within that mode. The Combined mode allows simultaneous
targeting of discourse frame and grammar structure.

INTERPRETIVE RULE — ROLE TYPES:
  Always Active: evaluated on every response; cannot be turned off.
  Focus Mode: selected by instructor; affects feedback priority and adds
    scaffold_feedback field. Never affects scoring logic or holistic score.
  Sub-Target: selected within Discourse Frame, Grammar Structure, or Combined
    modes; specifies the particular frame or structure being targeted.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FIXED INFRASTRUCTURE — Always Active, Not Instructor-Configurable
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Reasoning Gate
  Level 3 reasoning evaluated on every response. Score cap rule never changes
  regardless of any instructor configuration. Cannot be disabled or adjusted.
  Gate failure — no developed reasoning move (speculation, consequence, or
  relationship) carries meaning in a way that can be followed from what is
  explicitly stated — caps at Score 2 regardless of all other criteria.

  Reasoning move taxonomy (always evaluated):
    SPECULATION: projects beyond what is known — hypothesizes about
      possibilities, future states, or scenarios not yet realized.
    CONSEQUENCES: traces what follows from a claim — effects, implications,
      outcomes, or systemic results beyond the first-order statement.
    RELATIONSHIPS: identifies how two or more things connect — causal,
      conditional, concessive, comparative, or stakeholder relationships
      structuring the argument.

  Invocation without development does not clear the gate. A label or partial
  statement without developed content ("this has consequences," "this affects
  society") is Not Realized regardless of grammatical complexity.

Level 3 Language and Discourse Baseline
  All three co-determinative criteria evaluated on every response regardless
  of focus mode selection: Level 3 reasoning, Level 3 language, Level 3
  discourse. Absence of Level 3 in any criterion caps the score regardless
  of strength in others. Absence means no identifiable Level 3 function —
  not imprecision or partial realization.

Scaffold Invariant Enforcement
  Holistic score produced against full rubric on every response regardless
  of focus mode. scaffold_feedback field added when focus mode is not Holistic.
  Holistic score never modified by scaffold selection.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FOCUS MODES — Instructor Selects One
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

HOLISTIC
  No scaffold target active. Scorer evaluates all three co-determinative
  criteria equally. scaffold_feedback field omitted from JSON output.
  Recommended for: diagnostic assessment, initial placement, and responses
  where the instructor wants an unweighted holistic evaluation.

ARGUMENT STRUCTURE
  No sub-target required. Feedback weighted toward the PSU arc:
    Position — clear stance stated
    Support — specific evidence or reasoning connected to position
    Analysis — developed reasoning move drawn from the support
  Scorer identifies which tier is absent, not realized, developing, or
  sustained and provides one targeted developmental suggestion for the
  tier where Level 3 breaks down.
  Recommended for: learners whose discourse frame and grammar structures
  are developing but whose overall argument architecture is the primary
  breakdown point; early ESO practice before frame-specific targeting.

DISCOURSE FRAME
  Sub-target required: instructor selects one of six frames (see Section 5).
  Feedback weighted toward whether the student deployed the selected frame
  as a developed reasoning move — not merely mentioned it.
  Scorer evaluates the frame for REDS status and provides one targeted
  developmental suggestion.
  Recommended for: learners who have argument structure developing but need
  to build specific reasoning frame repertoire; targeted practice on a
  frame introduced in class.

GRAMMAR STRUCTURE
  Sub-target required: instructor selects one of seven structures (see
  Section 6). Optional secondary target available.
  Feedback weighted toward whether the selected structure carries a reasoning
  move and is completed and functional — not merely whether the structure
  is present.
  Scorer evaluates the structure for REDS status and provides one targeted
  developmental suggestion per active target.
  Recommended for: learners whose reasoning moves are present but whose
  grammatical realization of those moves is developing; form-focused practice
  in service of argument function.

COMBINED
  Two sub-targets required: one discourse frame (primary) + one grammar
  structure (secondary).
  Feedback weighted toward discourse frame as primary target; grammar
  structure as secondary. One developmental suggestion per target.
  Scorer evaluates both for REDS status.
  Recommended for: learners at the Score 3/4 boundary whose argument
  architecture is solid but who need simultaneous development of a specific
  frame and its grammatical realization; advanced targeted practice.
  Caution: Combined mode produces the densest feedback. Not recommended
  for early-stage learners or Score 2 profiles — the dual target may
  overwhelm rather than focus.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROMPT DIFFICULTY — Set at Task Design Level — Not a Scaffold Category
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Topic Domain
  Controls construct demand through topic selection:
  - Standard: familiar policy and social issues (education, environment, health)
  - Slight stretch: governance, civic, or institutional issues requiring
    stakeholder-level reasoning (regulation, public policy, military readiness)
  - Higher stretch: abstract or values-based issues requiring sustained
    Level 3 reasoning across competing frameworks (justice, ethics, sovereignty)

  Lexical range is not a scaffold focus category and is not instructor-
  configurable within the scaffold.

5. DISCOURSE FRAME SUB-TARGETS

Six frames available for Discourse Frame and Combined modes.
Each frame is evaluated for Level 3 functional presence using REDS status.
Level 3 present = frame deployed as a developed reasoning move followable
from what is explicitly stated. Level 3 not present = frame invoked as a
label without developed reasoning behind it.

5.1 Trade-offs & Constraints
  Level 3 present when: argument traces competing priorities with developed
  consequence or relationship reasoning across short-term/long-term or across
  stakeholder groups. Genuine tension acknowledged and developed — not one
  side stated and the other dismissed.
  Not Realized when: one side stated without developed contrast; competing
  priorities named but not analyzed.
  Pedagogic note: The most accessible entry point for Level 3 reasoning at
  ILR 2+. Trade-off framing is teachable and produces visible gains quickly.
  Recommended as a first Discourse Frame target for developing learners.

5.2 Scale & Stakeholder
  Level 3 present when: argument moves between individual and societal level
  with developed relationship reasoning — names stakeholder groups and connects
  consequences to each with specificity. Not present when: stays at one scale
  only, or stakeholder groups named without connecting consequences to them.
  Pedagogic note: Pairs naturally with Trade-offs & Constraints. Good second
  target once trade-off framing is developing.

5.3 Causal Systems
  Level 3 present when: argument traces cause-and-effect chains beyond the
  first step — consequence reasoning extending at least two steps; unintended
  consequences acknowledged or projected. Not present when: first-order
  consequence only, or consequence invoked without development.
  Pedagogic note: The most demanding frame for most learners. Causal chain
  reasoning requires sustained vocabulary control at elevation points.
  Recommended for learners at the Score 3/4 boundary.

5.4 Hypothetical & Conditional
  Level 3 present when: argument uses developed speculation — if/then reasoning
  projecting possibilities in functional conditional structures across the
  argument. Conditional must carry developed consequence or possibility, not
  merely restate the opening premise in conditional form.
  Not Realized when: one conditional sentence at opening only; conditional
  structures initiated and abandoned; conditional carries premise restatement
  rather than developed projection.
  Pedagogic note: The most common source of invocation inflation at the 2/3
  boundary. Grammatically functional conditionals that carry only premise
  restatement are Not Realized. See ESO scoring prompt named examples.

5.5 Value-based Evaluation
  Level 3 present when: argument identifies competing values with developed
  relationship reasoning — tension between values acknowledged and evaluated;
  does not resolve by dismissing one value entirely.
  Not present when: one value asserted without engaging tension; competing
  values named but not analyzed.
  Pedagogic note: Natural fit for topics with ethical or rights-based
  dimensions. Pairs well with Grammar Structure: Concession & Contrast.

5.6 Synthesis & Judgment
  Level 3 present when: argument draws on multiple developed reasoning moves
  and arrives at a reasoned position that accounts for complexity — closing
  advances beyond opening position with developed analytical movement.
  Not present when: closing merely restates opening; synthesis invoked but
  not developed.
  Pedagogic note: Score 3/4 boundary target. Requires sustained control
  across the full response. Not recommended as a first Discourse Frame
  target. Best deployed after Trade-offs, Scale & Stakeholder, or Causal
  Systems are developing.

================================================================================

6. GRAMMAR STRUCTURE SUB-TARGETS

Seven structures available for Grammar Structure and Combined modes.
Each structure is evaluated for Level 3 functional presence — not mere
presence. A structure that is initiated and abandoned, or present but not
carrying a reasoning move, is Not Realized.

6.1 Conditional Systems
  Level 3 present when: if/then constructions carry developed speculation or
  consequence reasoning; real, unreal, or mixed conditionals sustained across
  the argument, not limited to opening only. Conditional must carry developed
  content — not premise restatement.
  Pairs naturally with: Hypothetical & Conditional frame.

6.2 Concession & Contrast
  Level 3 present when: although, while, even though — carrying developed
  relationship reasoning; genuine tension acknowledged; structure completed
  and the rebuttal or contrast developed with specificity.
  Not Realized when: concessive structure initiated but rebuttal is bare
  assertion; one side acknowledged without developed contrast.
  Pairs naturally with: Trade-offs & Constraints, Value-based Evaluation.

6.3 Relative & Embedded Clauses
  Level 3 present when: which, that, who clauses carry a reasoning move —
  extend or qualify a claim in a way that advances the argument. Not merely
  decorative or additive.
  Not Realized when: relative clauses present but functioning as description
  rather than argument extension.
  Pairs naturally with: Causal Systems, Scale & Stakeholder.

6.4 Modality & Hedging
  Level 3 present when: may, might, could, arguably — carrying developed
  speculation; purposeful and distinguishing certain from probable; sustained
  across speculative moves in the argument.
  Not Realized when: modal forms used as filler or habit rather than
  purposeful hedging; not connected to developed speculation.
  Pairs naturally with: Hypothetical & Conditional frame.

6.5 Nominalization & Abstract Nouns
  Level 3 present when: verb/adjective → noun conversion carries policy or
  societal reasoning (implementation, regulation, distribution, deterioration);
  accurate in form; integrated into developed reasoning moves.
  Not Realized when: nominalized forms attempted but morphologically broken
  ("reproductive" for "reproduction"); invoked as vocabulary display without
  carrying reasoning.
  Pairs naturally with: Causal Systems, Scale & Stakeholder.

6.6 Passive & Reporting Structures
  Level 3 present when: passive voice and attributed reporting used
  purposefully for academic register; carries reasoning forward rather than
  functioning as avoidance of active construction.
  Not Realized when: passive used as avoidance without register function;
  reporting structures present but not advancing the argument.
  Pairs naturally with: Argument Structure focus mode.

6.7 Parallelism & Coordination
  Level 3 present when: balanced structures carry comparative or enumerative
  reasoning; elements grammatically matched; structure carries meaning rather
  than functioning as mere listing.
  Not Realized when: parallel structures present but elements unmatched or
  carrying no comparative/enumerative reasoning.
  Pairs naturally with: Trade-offs & Constraints, Synthesis & Judgment.

7. CONFIGURATION CONSTRAINTS AND INTERACTION RULES

7.1 Hard Rules

- Fixed infrastructure is always active. No instructor override.
- Reasoning gate failure caps at Score 2 regardless of focus mode selection.
- Instructor selects one and only one focus mode per task assignment.
- Discourse Frame mode requires one sub-target from Section 5.
- Grammar Structure mode requires one primary sub-target from Section 6;
  one optional secondary sub-target permitted.
- Combined mode requires one discourse frame sub-target and one grammar
  structure sub-target.
- Holistic mode: scaffold_feedback field omitted entirely from JSON output.
- Scaffold invariant: holistic score never modified by scaffold selection.
- PISU guidance must not appear in the same feedback block as holistic score
  justification. Developmental coaching only.
- Scoring outcomes are determined solely by construct criteria and gate rules.
  Instructor configuration affects feedback only.

7.2 Recommended and Cautioned Pairings

  Argument Structure — standalone
    Status: Recommended for early ESO practice
    Note: Best starting point before frame-specific targeting. Surfaces which
    PSU tier is the primary breakdown point without prescribing a specific
    reasoning frame.

  Discourse Frame: Trade-offs & Constraints + Grammar: Concession & Contrast
    Status: Recommended
    Note: Natural construct pairing — trade-off reasoning is most naturally
    realized through concessive grammatical structures. High alignment between
    frame and structure targets.

  Discourse Frame: Hypothetical & Conditional + Grammar: Conditional Systems
    Status: Recommended
    Note: Direct frame-structure alignment. Surfaces whether the student can
    realize hypothetical reasoning in functional conditional form.

  Discourse Frame: Causal Systems + Grammar: Relative & Embedded Clauses
    Status: Recommended
    Note: Causal chain reasoning is often realized through relative clauses
    that extend consequence moves. Good pairing for Score 3/4 boundary work.

  Discourse Frame: Scale & Stakeholder + Grammar: Nominalization
    Status: Recommended
    Note: Societal-level reasoning typically requires nominalized vocabulary.
    Pairing surfaces whether vocabulary control supports the scale move.

  Discourse Frame: Synthesis & Judgment + Grammar: Parallelism
    Status: Recommended for Score 3/4 boundary
    Note: Advanced pairing. Synthesis reasoning is often realized through
    balanced comparative structures. For learners with solid Score 3 profiles.

  Combined mode + Topic Domain (higher stretch)
    Status: Caution
    Note: Cognitive load warning displayed at assignment creation. Dual
    scaffold target plus high topic demand may produce breakdown that reflects
    topic difficulty rather than scaffold target weakness. Diagnostic use only.
    Cognitive load warning text: "Combined scaffold mode and high topic demand
    are both active. Breakdown in this configuration may reflect topic
    complexity rather than control of the targeted frame or structure.
    Interpret scaffold feedback cautiously."

  Combined mode + Score 2 profile learners
    Status: Not recommended
    Note: Combined mode produces the densest feedback. Score 2 learners need
    first to develop a single reasoning move to functional presence. Dual
    targeting before Level 3 is functionally present is unlikely to produce
    actionable gains.

8. SUBCATEGORY PROGRESSIONS BY FOCUS MODE

8.1 Argument Structure Focus

  Absent
    No identifiable position present. Response consists of restatement of
    prompt content or disconnected statements. PSU arc not initiated.
    Level 3 not functionally present. ILR 2.

  Not Realized
    Position stated but argument does not develop. Support present but not
    connected to position. Analysis tier invoked but not developed — reasoning
    move attempted and abandoned. Level 3 not functionally present. ILR 2.

  Developing
    Position stated and identifiable. Support connected to position. At least
    one developed reasoning move present in the analysis tier — consequence,
    speculation, or relationship followable from what is explicitly stated.
    Breakdown expected at higher demand moments. Level 3 functionally present
    but inconsistent. ILR 2+.

  Sustained
    Position stated and maintained without drift. Support developed and
    connected. Multiple reasoning moves distributed across the response.
    Argumentative arc advances coherently from warrant to implication.
    Level 3 sustained and controlled. ILR 3.

8.2 Discourse Frame Focus — General Progression

  Absent
    Selected frame not present or not attempted. Argument does not deploy
    the targeted reasoning pattern. ILR 2.

  Not Realized
    Frame invoked as a label but not developed. Structural markers present
    (e.g., conditional form, concessive opener) but reasoning behind the
    structure not populated. Listener must supply logical steps. ILR 2.

  Developing
    Frame deployed as a developed reasoning move in at least one instance.
    Followable from what is explicitly stated. Inconsistent — frame present
    in some portions, absent or Not Realized in others. Breakdown at
    elevation points expected. ILR 2+.

  Sustained
    Frame deployed as a developed reasoning move consistently across the
    response. Distributed and stable under task demands. ILR 3.

  NOTE: Frame-specific Level 3 presence/absence criteria are defined in
  Section 5 for each of the six frames.

8.3 Grammar Structure Focus — General Progression

  Absent
    Selected structure not present or not attempted. ILR 2.

  Not Realized
    Structure initiated but not completed; or present in form but not
    carrying a reasoning move; or morphologically broken at elevation
    points. ILR 2.

  Developing
    Structure present and carrying a reasoning move in at least one instance.
    Functional — followable from what is explicitly stated. Inconsistent —
    present in some places, absent or Not Realized in others. ILR 2+.

  Sustained
    Structure deployed consistently across the response. Load-bearing —
    carries reasoning moves throughout. Stable under task demands. ILR 3.

  NOTE: Structure-specific Level 3 presence/absence criteria are defined
  in Section 6 for each of the seven structures.

9. OPEN ITEMS AND PHASE 2 DECISIONS

9.1 Argument Structure Focus — Phase 2 Checkpoint

  Retain in MVP. After early deployment, evaluate:
  - Is Argument Structure feedback distinct enough from Holistic feedback
    to justify as a separate focus mode?
  - Do instructors select it? Or do they move directly to frame/structure
    targeting?
  - Decision rule: If redundant with Holistic → retire or merge.
    If instructors use it to target PSU arc specifically → keep.

9.2 Combined Mode — Monitor Cap

  Combined mode has two active targets by definition. Evaluate in Phase 2:
  - Does dual-target feedback produce actionable output or diffuse feedback?
  - Is the discourse frame / grammar structure weighting ratio correct?
  - Should Combined mode be restricted to Score 3+ profiles only?

9.3 Grammar Structure Secondary Target

  Grammar Structure mode permits one optional secondary target. Evaluate
  in Phase 2:
  - Does dual grammar structure targeting produce useful output?
  - Is this being used? If not, consider retiring the optional secondary
    slot for Grammar Structure mode (it is required in Combined mode).

9.4 Disfluency at ILR 3

  ESO scoring relies on transcript-visible disfluency signals only. The
  three-level continuum (Minimal / Moderate / Numerous) that governs ILR 2
  task scoring is not applied to ESO tasks in the same form. The ESO disfluency
  signal is structural collapse at ceiling moments — false starts revealing
  inability to complete Level 3 structures. Monitor in deployment:
  - Are there ESO responses where delivery-rate disfluency (not structural
    collapse) is affecting perceived Level 3 presence?
  - If so, consider whether a moderate disfluency cap at Score 4 (not Score 3)
    is warranted for ESO tasks.

10.2 Key Governance Decisions from Norming
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  INVOCATION INFLATION — PRIMARY VALIDITY RISK:
  The most significant and recurring scoring error at the 2/3 boundary.
  Grammatically functional structures (conditionals, concessives) that carry
  only premise restatement or bare assertion behind them are Not Realized —
  not Developing. The test is always: can the reasoning be followed from
  what is explicitly stated without supplying unstated logical steps?

  BREVITY DOES NOT DETERMINE SCORE:
  Confirmed by T3 (Score 3, brief) vs. T12 (Score 2, brief). A brief response
  with one genuine developed reasoning move clears Score 3. A brief response
  with policy vocabulary but no developed reasoning move does not.

  GRAMMATICAL COMPLEXITY ≠ REASONING MOVE:
  Confirmed by Geoengineering and Basic Income (both Score 2 despite complex
  structures). Complex grammatical form is necessary but not sufficient for
  Level 3. The reasoning content behind the structure must be developed.

  ACCURACY ERRORS DO NOT CAP SCORE 3:
  Confirmed by T8 (Score 3 despite accuracy errors). Accuracy is a Score 3/4
  discriminator, not a Score 2/3 discriminator. Article control and morphological
  precision are Score 4 features.

  "SUSTAINED" RESERVED FOR SCORE 4:
  The REDS term "sustained" must not appear in Score 3 feedback commentary.
  At Score 3 use "developing," "functional," or "present but inconsistent."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10.3 Snorkl Validity Notes — ESO Task
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  SCORE LABEL FORMAT: Snorkl uses fractional and quality-gradient labels
  ("3/4 — Strong", "2/4 — Fair"). B10-PP uses integer + rubric label only
  ("3 — Good"). Snorkl label format is non-construct — exclude from score
  comparison. Compare integer scores only.

  TASK COMPLIANCE BUNDLING: Snorkl feedback referencing PISU completion,
  response length, or missing structural elements as language score factors
  should be excluded from score comparison. These are non-construct under
  REDS.

  FLUENCY OVERCREDITING: Snorkl shows a pattern of crediting fluent delivery
  and extended length as evidence of Level 3 presence. Under REDS, fluency
  and length are explicitly not scored. A fluent, extended response built on
  assertion and listing is Score 2.

  RECOMMENDATION: Treat Snorkl 3/4 and 4/4 on ESO tasks as requiring expert
  review before confirmation as Score 3 or Score 4 anchors. The overcrediting
  pattern is consistent with Snorkl's behavior on ILR 2 tasks.
