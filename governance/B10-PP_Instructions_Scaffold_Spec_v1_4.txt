1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This document specifies the scaffold configuration system for instruction-giving
tasks in the B10 Practice Platform (B10-PP). The scaffold allows instructors to
configure AI scoring and feedback focus for individual task assignments targeting
ILR 1 to ILR 2 speaking proficiency.

Scope: Instruction-giving tasks only. ILR 2 is the performance target. ILR 1 is
the assumed floor. ILR 3 constructs are explicitly out of scope.

Task definition: The student gives a cohesive sequence of steps for a familiar or
routine process in dependency-correct order. Examples include using self-checkout,
getting a driver's license, making a favorite dish, preparing for a test, or
enrolling in a course.

PEDAGOGIC RATIONALE: The scaffold serves two functions. For the learner, it
isolates sub-skills for focused practice — a learner cannot improve everything
at once, and a tractable target produces faster, more visible gains. For the
platform, it makes explicit that B10-PP teaches procedural language use in
context — how to organize a sequence, how to frame steps as directives, how to
connect moves into a followable discourse. This is transferable language function,
not test preparation.

2. DESIGN PRINCIPLES

- Fixed infrastructure and instructor-configurable focus are strictly separated.
  Construct-critical rules are never instructor-modifiable.

- Primary focus drives feedback priority. The scorer leads with the
  instructor-selected dimension.

- A monitor is a performance feature that the scoring engine tracks and reports
  on in feedback output. Monitors do not affect scoring, do not modify the gate
  criterion, and do not determine the primary feedback focus. They surface
  targeted observational data alongside the primary focus feedback.

- Sequential Logic gate is always active and never configurable. Selecting
  Process Organization as a primary focus affects feedback emphasis only —
  not scoring logic. Gate failure dominates feedback regardless of focus
  selection.

- Present tense and imperative are the expected primary forms for instruction-
  giving tasks. Directive modals (you need to, you should, you have to) count
  toward directive function. Past tense appropriate only for anecdotal inserts
  that frame or motivate an imperative step.

- Technical expertise about the described process is not required and is not
  scored. Executability is evaluated relative to reasonable familiarity only.

- Lexical range is managed through topic domain selection at task design, not
  through a scaffold focus category.

- Disfluency monitoring does not change the scoring cap rule. It shifts feedback
  priority only.

- Scoring outcomes are determined solely by construct criteria and gate rules.
  Instructor configuration affects feedback only.

3. REDS FRAMEWORK AND SCORING ALIGNMENT

REDS FRAMEWORK — FUNCTIONAL PERFORMANCE STATES

Absent:
  The target feature is not present.

Not Realized:
  The target feature is attempted but does not functionally materialize.
  - attempted but not functional
  - invoked but not developed
  - does not carry meaning
  - requires inference from the listener to complete

Developing:
  The target feature is functionally present.
  - functionally present
  - followable from what is explicitly stated
  - inconsistent
  - breaks under pressure or across parts of the response

Sustained:
  The target feature is controlled across the full response.
  - controlled
  - distributed across the response
  - stable under task demands

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SCORING ALIGNMENT — ILR 2 TARGET

Score 3 corresponds to Sustained performance.
Developing performance does not meet the threshold for Score 3.
Not Realized performance does not meet the threshold for Score 2.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FUNCTIONAL THRESHOLD

A feature is considered functionally realized when it carries meaning in a way
that can be followed from what is explicitly stated, without requiring the
listener to infer missing steps or structure.

4. SCAFFOLD ARCHITECTURE

INTERPRETIVE RULE — ROLE TYPES:
  Always Active: evaluated on every response; cannot be turned off.
  Primary Focus: selected by instructor; affects feedback priority only.
  Monitor: optional visibility layer; does not affect scoring or gate logic.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAYER 1 — Fixed Infrastructure — Always Active, Not Instructor-Configurable
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Sequential Logic Gate
  Dependency-correct sequencing evaluated on every response. Score cap rule
  never changes regardless of any instructor configuration. Cannot be disabled
  or adjusted. Gate failure — steps not in dependency-correct order or
  semantically inconsistent with the named process — caps at Score 1 regardless
  of all other criteria.

Disfluency Default
  Disfluency monitored on every response using a three-level continuum.
  This behavior is always active and is never overridden or replaced by any
  instructor configuration. Selecting Fluency Focus as a monitor increases
  feedback priority for disfluency patterns — it does not change monitoring
  behavior, scoring logic, or cap thresholds.

  Three-level continuum:
    Minimal: Occasional false starts or filled pauses. Does not penalize
      Score 2 or 3.
    Moderate: Noticeable false starts, repairs, or pauses distributed across
      the response. A sympathetic listener can follow but a non-sympathetic
      listener would struggle. Caps at Score 2 regardless of functional
      criterion performance.
    Numerous: Pervasive false starts or pauses that fragment delivery and
      impede meaning. Caps at Score 1 regardless of all other criteria.

  Disfluency metadata (silent pause counts, mean inter-word gap) is passed
  to the scoring engine alongside the raw transcript. ILR threshold guidance
  for metadata interpretation is included in Prompt 4d.

Directive Function Default
  Directive function evaluated on every response regardless of primary focus
  selection. Always reported in feedback when absence or weakness is a scoring
  factor.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAYER 2 — Primary Focus — Instructor Selects One
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Process Organization Focus
  Feedback emphasis on whether the steps are correct, complete, and in the right
  order. Targets the full arc of the process — are the right steps present,
  sequenced correctly, and sufficient for a generally familiar listener to execute
  without inference? Draws from Sequential Logic (gate) and Step Completeness
  criteria. When gate fails, feedback prioritizes process organization regardless
  of focus selection.
  Levels: unexecutable / partially organized / complete and dependency-correct.

  Recommended for: early-stage learners building process knowledge; learners
  whose directive language is developing but whose process sequencing is the
  primary breakdown point.

Directive Language Focus
  Feedback emphasis on whether the student is operating in directive mode and
  constructing steps as executable actions. Targets imperative form realization,
  directive modal use, and the distinction between directive moves and
  observational or narrative framing. Draws from Directive Function and Formal
  Control criterion.
  Levels: absent / developing / sustained norm.

  Recommended for: learners whose process knowledge is solid but who default
  to descriptive or narrative framing rather than directive construction.

Discourse Cohesion Focus
  Feedback emphasis on how steps are connected into a followable sequence.
  Targets signal word use, step-level chaining, and whether the response holds
  together as a unified discourse rather than a list. Draws from Step Completeness
  criterion cohesion dimension. Distinct from Process Organization (which targets
  content correctness) — a response can be correctly organized with weak cohesion,
  or smoothly connected with organizational gaps.
  Levels: disconnected / partially connected / controlled throughout.

  Recommended for: learners approaching the Score 2/3 boundary whose process
  and directive function are developing but whose discourse-level integration
  is the remaining gap.

  NOTE: Discourse Cohesion is available as Layer 2 primary focus only — not as
  a Layer 3 monitor. Signal Word Control monitor handles the surface-observable
  subset of cohesion at the monitor layer.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAYER 3 — Optional Monitors — Instructor Selects Any Combination
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Signal Word Control
  Monitors use and distribution of transition markers (first, next, then, after
  that, finally, once, before, when). Targets whether signal words are present,
  whether they are distributed across the full response, and whether they
  accurately mark step boundaries. Selection increases feedback prominence for
  signal word patterns — it does not affect scoring.

  Pedagogic note: Signal words are the most immediately teachable and improvable
  surface feature of instruction-giving. Focused practice on transition markers
  produces visible gains quickly — high motivational value for L2 learners at
  the ILR 1+/2 boundary.

Fluency Focus
  Monitors false starts, repetition, filled pauses, and hesitation patterns.
  Shifts feedback priority toward disfluency patterns. Does NOT change the
  scoring cap rule — the three-level continuum applies regardless of this
  setting. Selection increases feedback prominence for disfluency — it does
  not affect cap thresholds or scoring outcomes.

Basic Structures
  Monitors: present tense consistency, imperative morphology (add vs. adding
  vs. you add), subject-verb agreement, basic word order. These features are
  directly observable and directly tied to the directive function construct at
  ILR 1-2. Article errors, preposition errors, and complex clause errors are
  excluded as ILR 3+ concerns. Selection increases feedback prominence for
  these features — it does not activate new scoring criteria.

  NOTE: All three monitors are available for simultaneous selection. Activating
  all three may produce diffuse feedback. Recommended maximum: two active
  monitors per assignment.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROMPT DIFFICULTY — Set at Task Design Level — Not a Scaffold Category
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Topic Domain
  Controls process complexity through topic selection:
  - Standard: familiar personal processes (making a dish, preparing for a test,
    using self-checkout)
  - Slight stretch: procedural civic or institutional processes (getting a
    driver's license, opening a bank account, enrolling in a course)
  - Higher stretch: multi-phase professional or technical processes (filing a
    government form, applying for a security clearance, conducting a military
    inspection)

5. CONFIGURATION CONSTRAINTS AND INTERACTION RULES

5.1 Hard Rules

- Layer 1 is always active. No instructor override.
- Instructor selects one and only one primary focus from Layer 2.
- Sequential Logic gate failure caps at Score 1 regardless of primary focus
  selection. Process Organization focus increases feedback density on
  gate-adjacent issues on passing responses only.
- Discourse Cohesion is Layer 2 only — not available as a Layer 3 monitor.
- Scoring outcomes are determined solely by construct criteria and gate rules.
  Instructor configuration affects feedback only.

5.2 Recommended and Cautioned Pairings

  Process Organization + Signal Word Control
    Status: Recommended
    Note: Best pairing for early-stage learners — content correctness and
    surface cohesion markers together. High actionability.

  Directive Language + Fluency Focus
    Status: Recommended
    Note: Production load focus — for learners whose process knowledge is
    solid but whose directive construction breaks down under fluency pressure.

  Discourse Cohesion + Signal Word Control
    Status: Recommended
    Note: Score 2/3 boundary target — discourse integration and its most
    observable surface marker together.

  Topic Domain (stretch) + Fluency Focus
    Status: Caution
    Note: Cognitive load warning displayed; diagnostic use only.

  Cognitive load warning text (displayed at assignment creation):
  "High domain load and fluency monitoring are both active. Breakdown in this
  configuration may reflect topic complexity rather than fluency control.
  Interpret fluency data cautiously."

6. SUBCATEGORY PROGRESSIONS BY LAYER 2 FOCUS

6.1 Process Organization Focus

  Unexecutable
    Steps missing, out of order, or semantically inconsistent with the named
    process. A generally familiar listener cannot follow or execute. ILR 1.

  Partially Organized
    Overall process direction correct. Major steps present. Some ordering gaps
    or missing steps. Listener can follow with effort and some inference. ILR 1+.

  Complete and Dependency-Correct
    All major steps present and in dependency-correct sequence. No critical
    gaps. Listener can execute without inference. ILR 2.

6.2 Directive Language Focus

  Absent
    Directive function not present or not the tendency. Steps delivered as
    observations, descriptions, future predictions, or past-tense narrative.
    Does not function as instruction-giving. ILR 1.

  Developing
    Directive function the tendency — present in more step positions than absent.
    Imperative form and/or directive modals present but inconsistent. Non-directive
    framing in step positions recurring but not dominant. ILR 1+.

  Sustained Norm
    Directive function the sustained norm across executable steps. Imperative
    form realized in the clear majority of step positions. Non-imperative forms
    in functional supporting roles only. ILR 2.

6.3 Discourse Cohesion Focus

  Disconnected
    Steps listed without connective tissue. No signal words or transitional
    devices. Response functions as a bare list rather than a connected sequence.
    ILR 1.

  Partially Connected
    Some signal words present. Basic transitions mark some step boundaries.
    Cohesion develops inconsistently — present in some portions, absent in
    others. Overall sequence direction recoverable. ILR 1+.

  Controlled Throughout
    Signal words controlled and distributed across the full response. Step-level
    chaining present. Response holds together as a unified discourse. Listener
    can follow transitions without inferring connections. ILR 2.

7. NORMING SESSION STATUS

Norming session completed April 2026. Twelve transcripts scored across three
task prompts (bank account, job interview, study for test, self-checkout x4,
high-volume schedule). Inter-rater comparison with Snorkl AI throughout.

7.1 Confirmed Anchors

  Score 3 — signal words sparse, step-chaining sufficient (self-checkout)
  Score 3 — all criteria sustained, signal words controlled (study for test)
  Score 3 — strong coverage, outcome statements functional (self-checkout)
  Score 2 — signal words strong, steps partially complete (bank account)
  Score 2 — macro-sequence followable, directive function not sustained (job interview)
  Score 2 — future will dominance, scanning phase absent (self-checkout)
  Score 2 — high content awareness, future will/going to, verbose (self-checkout)
  Score 2 — functionally Score 3, held at Score 2 by moderate disfluency cap
  Score 2 — transcript scores 3, audio pacing caps at Score 2 (pipeline validation)
  Score 2/3 boundary — resolved Score 2 (job interview)
  Score 1/2 boundary — future will dominant, low directive presence
  Score 1 — semantic inconsistency gate failure (self-checkout weight device)

7.2 Open Anchors

  Score 1 — disfluency cap (Score 1 level): pending STT pipeline deployment.
  Moderate disfluency cap (Score 2): confirmed in norming via expert audio
  judgment; pending automated metadata validation in deployment.

7.3 Snorkl Validity Notes

  Snorkl systematically overcalls at 3/4 and 4/4 on instructions task.
  Root cause: credits signal word presence and content elaboration as
  sufficient for Score 3 without requiring sustained directive function.
  Imperative presence treated as equivalent to imperative as norm. Register
  sophistication suggestions appear as construct feedback — not valid.
  Snorkl also shows score drift behavior on Score 1 responses — initial
  correct gate failure call revised upward after feedback generation.
  Recommendation: Treat Snorkl 3/4 and 4/4 on instructions as requiring
  expert review before confirmation as Score 3 anchors. Treat any Snorkl
  score revision as requiring expert review.
