This is not a proposal. It is the next iteration of a system already working — built by the same instructor, using the same methodology, at greater scale.
1
The Problem Leadership Already Owns
DLIELC's throughput targets depend on consistent, measurable progress across a distributed instructional environment where students rotate through multiple instructors weekly — sometimes six or more in a single week. Historical performance data rarely follows the student. Each new instructor starts without context. Session time is lost to orientation rather than instruction. Continuity is informal, verbal, and inconsistent.
2
The Solution Already Being Built
B10 Practice Platform creates a persistent performance record anchored to the student — not the roster, not the class, not the instructor. Students carry a B10 ID. Any instructor who works with them that week — main class or supplemental — looks up the student in seconds and sees the complete arc: what was practiced, how they scored, what Claude flagged, what every prior instructor noted. No admin burden. No roster management. No CUI risk. The student's history travels with them automatically.
3
What This Means for the Program
Every instructor who touches a student in a given week works from the same current picture. Session ramp-up time is reduced. Supplemental language training becomes additive rather than redundant. Progress is visible at the program level for the first time — not inferred from interview outcomes, but tracked continuously across every practice session. The platform does not replace instructor judgment. It informs it.

The Ask
The core platform reaches its first student-facing milestone in July 2026. A pilot cohort of 2–3 main class instructors and 1–2 SLT instructors will validate the system in real instructional conditions over approximately one month.

The request is not for funding or resources. It is for 30 minutes to walk through pilot results when they are available — and to discuss whether formal program adoption makes sense in the context of DLIELC's throughput and continuity priorities.
