Trade-offs Focus
You identified a clear short-term concern when you said "initially this reduces office costs" — strong opening to trade-off reasoning. The long-term analysis was brief. You moved to your conclusion without exploring unintended consequences or stakeholder impact over time.
What you did well
You accurately conveyed the central issue and used hedging naturally: "this could potentially lead to" and "it appears that" both appeared. Your concessive clause "although productivity may increase" was well-formed and added nuance.
What to work on
Push further into the long-term tier of your trade-off analysis. Ask: what happens two or three steps downstream? Who benefits and who is disadvantaged over time? The passage's point about distributed workforce inequality was not addressed.
Language note
Consider using nominalization to increase abstraction — "the distribution of work" rather than "when people work from different places."
What we heard you say
The passage discusses whether remote work should remain standard. The speaker argues that although productivity may increase, it could potentially lead to greater inequality. Initially this reduces office costs but there are long-term concerns...
